Sure!” He had waited a long time for this, his machinations, his support of the Greywoods, his disposal of the last Wrath, it had all led to this moment. He had paid a price for his weasley betrayal of Wrath, the last action of the previous pillar had been to injure his brain in such a way that he could only talk in rhyme. “Chase Grorp. Do you accept the mantle that will be laid upon you?” Jiran Greywood lifted the helm over Grorp’s head.

“I will.” he said, his words echoing in the empty room. The realisation of the position was making him as excited as when two cousins meet for the first time in South Carolina. Almost as excited as when he killed for money, or for imagined slight, or just because he wanted to. His words echoed in the chamber that was empty except for himself and the Greywoods.

Jiran brough the bear helm down onto his head, which squeaked unceremoniously as it went over his large ears. “Then rise, our new Wrath.”

Umbra Greywood pressed a button on the intercom embedded in the dias. “Send them in please.”

The large steel doors to the chamber opened inwards with an excruciating slow movement. Once clear, the other Pillars walked in somberly, then stopped at the sight of the man in the bear helm.

“Meet your new Wrath.” Jiran announced.

The Ward, the education Pillar in the owl helm, was the first to break the shocked hush. “The investigation is not complete on the previous Wrath yet, aren’t we jumping the gun a little?”

“Wrath was a traitor, and at this time of war we need stability and power.” Umbra said beside Jiran, her old lady demeanour slightly gone. She was standing upright, confident. “You six will be needed in the coming days. Seven, once we call in Wraith.”

The Lion Helm of the Will, the religious leader, stepped forward.“Of course we’ll be needed, we will have the defence of Ilima to consider.” She spoke with a disdain she normally hid from authority figures. Even she was irritated with the situation, it didn’t matter how much she despised Wrath, it was like losing a sibling you hated. It was complicated.

Jiran held up a hand. “You will be needed to hunt Kellan Amray and her band of traitors through the time stream, and bring her back unspoiled.”

“The Pillars have autonomy in the work that they choose to do” The Writ, the law Pillar in the fox helm said. He was incredulous. “We’re not a hammer to be wielded. We are here for the betterment of society.”

Jiran stepped down from the dias and rounded the stone structure to stand alongside Grorp. “That was before one of you decided to kill General Amray. You are here for the betterment of Ilima. And now you are answerable to Wrath, who answers to us.”

Wrath stepped forward and lifted his helm, to the gasps of the other Pillars. Removing your helm was sacrosanct. “Grorp?” The Way, the science pillar in the stag helm, summed up the groups astonishment at their old manservant becoming their peer - not to mention his lack of hobbling and bending. “How is this possible?”

“The wheel that spins has many teeth,

And karma stings those underneath.

When lines are toed and dues arranged

It will be easier to accept the change.”

Group smirked and walked through the group, leaving them looking at each other in shock.

Asher proclaimed the killings were all located in the vicinity of this…Poe Creek Park. We need to find the culprits, or Kellan and Florgo will rot in a…HUM13, what is this?”

HUM13 bent down. “Looks like large foot prints to me, and you know what they say about men with big feet…do you know, because I am unable to find any congruent data on this analogy? Would you like me to scan for…”

A resonant clang sounded, and HUM13 collapsed next to Oryx, instantly deactivated. Oryx spun to find a group of ape looking humanoids with clubs.

One of them smugly put down his cards before moving a small piece on the board.

“And that’s the game fellows.” He said, and Oryx noticed he had a small bowler hat atop his head.

One with a monocle stepped forward. “Hello sir, I’m sorry we have been forced to detain you. We just can’t have the local constabulary poking their noses into our affairs until we’re ready to reveal ourselves.”

To their credit, none of the Bigfeet (I’m going with Bigfeet, it just sounds better) seemed shocked, they just nodded wholeheartedly in agreement.

Then she realised, she didn’t have to open a big portal. She concentrated and then relaxed, and suddenly Gooch stopped in his tracks. He drooled profusely, his eyes glazing over as if her were drunk, then he fell face forwards into the mud.

“Tally-ho! Her Majesty’s Bigfeet Brigade are here!” A voice sounded as Oryx and HUM13, along with four ape men, burst through the treeline only to then stand around with nothing to do.

“Guest starring HUM13 and Oryx!” announced HUM13.

Oryx sighed, and went to Kellan to untie her. “We arrived as expeditiously as we could. The Bigfeet needed to prepare their battle cries, then they had a vote, and it turned out to be a contentious issue. I take it that was our killer, whatever did you do to him?”

Kellan pushed the ropes off and hugged Oryx in thanks. “I realised a small portal could do just as much damage as a big one in the right area, so I Rifted a section of his brain about fifty miles north.”

“God bless us, every one!” HUM13 announced carrying Hallgrim.

It was a few hours before Crawford brought Asher and Florgo back from the precinct, and to his credit, Crawford only screamed and pointed his gun and accidentally firing it at the sight of the four Bigfeet, which marked him as one of the greatest policemen that ever lived.

Kellan had been explaining that the likelihood of the Bigfeet being caught if they stayed together was four times as likely, but the men were adamant they stay together. As she sent them to a more remote part of the United States, they all exited with choruses of “Cheerio and Ta-Ta!”

Crawford brought Asher to one side and kept his voice low. “I assume you have to go?”

“Yes, w..we have other places, other timelines to visit.” Asher kept her eyes down, not wanting to look him in the face. “How will you explain this?”

He smirked. “Aneurysm? His prints are over the knife and the cut patterns are from that knife clearly. Might be in line for a promotion thanks to you guys.”

Asher kissed him on the cheek. “You’re better at this than you think, Brett Crawford. Keep helping people, the police need more men like you.”

“You ever come back to this timeline, you look me up, you hear?” Crawford said as a Kellan opened a Rift behind them.

Asher nodded, and walked over to her sister, a tear in her eye.

Hallgrim nudged Florgo. “Whatever happened with the police chief?”

“I though he was suspicious. He had the gait, the forehead, the hair and general demeanour of a viscous cryptid. He was angry and sweaty, furious at my existence and threatened to kill me at any point.” Forgo shrugged. “Turns out he was just a usual Policeman.”

They all walked through the portal and Kellan closed it slowly behind them.

“God bless us every one!” HUM13 shouted. Oryx sighed again.
