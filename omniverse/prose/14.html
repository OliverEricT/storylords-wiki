The kid stumbled in the mud in front of Hallie and ate dirt, fully face planting the ground. She was black robed and black dressed, like she’d just come from a My Alchemical Romance concert. Without breaking stride, the Wood Elf swooped the girl up in her arms and used her sword, “The Fucker-Upper”, to chop clean through one of the trees in her path. It collapsed behind her, taking some of the chittinous, robotic demons pursuing them down with it, crushing their guts up through their heads like gross tubes of toothpaste - 9 out of 10 dentists would not have recommended it.

Hallie and her charge burst into the clearing, a vast, flat circle where there was no cover and nowhere for the two to hide. Perfect. This was it, where they’d fight. As the robotic monsters swarmed around them in the clearing, Hallie noticed that they were each composed of various different species - a clawed arm of a crustacean clan, the slick, hairless torso of a merman, the horse legs of a centaur (or maybe a horse, who could tell), the clapping buttcheeks of a redonkadonkula. Each part stitched together with the same black wires they had found around the countdown ‘tree’ at Gladecleft. They were monstrosities, disgusting abominations of the Technomancer that had been risen from the dead and revived by dark malice. Unthinking, unfeeling affronts to life that she’d happily put down. She shook her head, where had this rage come from? She had remained calm whilst her kin had been slaughtered by the Dankpit Dwarves, while she fought the Ithean wars once she’d joined the royal guard. She even had retained her composure when the Technomancer destroyed all the major cities of Ithea last month. She was - to all who knew her - cooler than a polar bear's testicles with a pair of aviator shades on. But recently she was fury - red hot, burning, don’t ticket me I’ve only been here for five minutes to drop something off, oh goddamn you, you’re ticketing me, why don’t you get a real job you jumped up Nazi, aggression personified.

One of the creatures opened their mouth wide, but what came out wasn’t their voice, it was a chilling whisper that was somehow a hiss, quiet but somehow burning. “Give in, Swiftfeather. I have seen your slaughter at my hands. It is glorious.”

Hallie sneered. “When I die I’ll be surrounded by comatose men, be it in bed or on the battlefield, it won’t be due to some tin can who thinks they’re a demon lord.” Suddenly she threw the young child at the lead cyberzombie, who recoiled at her apparent callousness (obvious he did not have children of his own). As the child flew through the air, it became a black wraith that abandoned human shape and enveloped the beast, tearing chunks off it until it was a quivering pile of machinery.

The Darkling, Taber, took his humanoid form again, inky black arms stretching. “And they say kids are a burden.” (Editors note: Kids are indeed a burden. A horrible, life-sucking burden.)

Bar’Thur and Blotch jumped down from the surrounding trees, Blotch dousing a few of the robots in her jellyskele acidic insides, as the force from her frogman friend’s cudgel demolished three more. Ria Grey, the human ranger, looked morose as she uncloaked behind one of the Technomancer’s zombies and slit the central fuel line in its neck, causing it to convulse and fall to the floor. Heath picked up the body with the strength expected from the son of an alternate reality Technomancer, and turned another few to dust. Doots played a lullabye that sent the other robots to sleep, meaning that the diminutive Mookie could finish them off by running around and unplugging them.

And quickly, they were alone in the clearing, all of them panting from the exhaustion of their ruse. Bar’Thur scratched at his neck, where his universal translator was buried just under the skin, his skin was dry and red and the scratching was unbearably loud.

Ria gritted her teeth then turned to him. “Damn you, stop that incessant scratching! You’re getting frog flakes everywhere.”

“Only if you stop that incessant whining, Grey.” Blotch stepped in as she always did, but this time it only made Bar’Thur angry. “I can fight my own battles, Blotch, especially against a sweaty Humie.”

It had been like this for weeks. With no sleep, no comfort, no respite - just the guilt they all felt at falling for the Technomancer’s obvious ruse to draw them out months ago. Before the Nightmare Council was formed, they had been plagued by evil visions, but this was even worse. They were all so raw and exposed, like a nudist in a sandpaper factory. As Doots brought her hand down from rubbing her itchy neck, she sneered at her fellows. “You’re all so quick to knife each other the moment we are finished fighting, can we not all take a moment’s peace?”

Mookie chimed in. “You could play something relaxing, it might help us…”

Doots turned her gaze on her friend. “We don’t all solve our problems via mental manipulation, Mookie.”

Mookie looked ashamed before Taber railed against Doots. “And they call me dark. Maybe you should remember it’s forgive and forget, bard, not forgive and twist the knife.”

All through this, the mechanical man, Heath, surveyed his new family and must have been wondering what he had let himself in for. At least he wasn’t fighting, Hallie sighed.

“Enough.” the Wood Elf finally said. “I know we’re at the end of our patience.I know we’re being hunted. But our people need us. We have found and liberated thousands of survivors these last four months, all we have left is to check the Capital for survivors, then we join the refugees at the docks, board the longboats and get the hell out of dodge. After we take the Technomancer down of course.”

The group fell silent, feeling the tide of shame when you are faced with the unkindnesses you have uttered, but also because of the mere mention of the Technomancer. They had voiced their reservations, the worries they couldn’t shake. It didn’t change their leader’s mind.

“Did anyone see how good I looked in that black dress?” Taber whistled. “I nearly fell in love with myself.”

Blotch chuckled. “You’re already in love with yourself Darkling, you just had a more reasonable excuse this time.”

Doots placed her head on Mookie’s shoulder, and Mookie brought a wing around their friend.

Heath crossed his legs and opened a compartment in his chest, bringing a bottle of sweet wine from a compartment within. “I found this in the Eleven settlement we saved three weeks ago. I was going to save it for when we…well…it seems more apropos now.”

He tossed the bottle to Bar’thur, who uncapped it with his cudgel, then brought the label to his face. “Centaur’s Stagger Runeshine - every mouthful equals one bad decision. Well we gots plenty of those ‘tween us already.” He took a swig, then handed it to Blotch. She poured it onto her front and absorbed it, the wine swirling in her transparent jelly and dissolving. “I’ve tasted better chariot fuel, but it’ll do the job.”

The bottle went round the group until emptied as they told stories. Taber told a tale of how he’d led a group of Darklings to victory against the Clan of Daves by pretending to be their shadows and infiltrating their camps. Ria told of hunting a whale by being swallowed and gradually cutting her way out with the corpse of an old wooden puppet. Doots played a piece she’d been writing called Silken Wing. But when Blotch begged Bar’Thur to tell a war story, he went quiet and sad.

“I been wanting to talk to you about those visions the Technomancer been feeding me. I fall in battle.” All went silent again, but Bar’Thur simply smiled. “Don’t you dare be sad. I wanted you to know that I’m not afraid of death, I’m not running from it, I’m with you all ‘til the end of the road.”

“Cause we all know how good frogs do with roads.” The group laughed, as Blotch lifted the bottle and poured a sip over her head. “We know that piece of crap planted all those dreams to pull us to Gladecleft, you need to leave that shit with that dumptruck ass of yours, man. Behind you.”

With the mood and spirits lifted, they slept, but not well. Despite, Blotch’s words, Ria Grey was assailed by the same nightmare she had had for a year, of a death she couldn’t fathom, couldn’t understand. In her sleep, she scratched at the glowing translator behind her ear.

“It’s still night.” Mookie mumbled, rubbing their eyes with their forelegs.

“Attempting to kill the Technomancer is folly.” He said, so direct and brazen that the rest of the group stopped what they were doing and fell silent. Blotch even put down the mutton leg she was disolving through her shell.

“Maybe you should.” Heath said calmly. “I am the son of a Technomancer, one that destroyed or subjugated every world he came into contact with. If his equivalent is here, in this universe, I have one piece of advice for you. Run. Find the deepest, darkest corner of the universe you can find and hide in it. He is a cancer, a horror, a plague of locusts come to destroy your harvest.”

Mookie watched as Hallie put down her rag, and set the shield down on the floor. She looked up at Heath, her eyes narrow, but her voice still calm. For now. “This Technomancer took everything from us, every person we knew, our lives. We didn’t grow up sitting on his lap, being coddled like a little Prince Droid. We fought to stop him.”

Heath was unphased. “And you failed, the same as I did.”

Hallie gathered her stuff and stood, rubbing at her neck as she did. “Not yet.”

The capital city of Etrus was once a gleaming bastion of interwoven nature, crystal and metal. The Great Tree of Harrad’Netarr, nearly a mile in height and with a root system that stretched even further, stood in the centre, its large, chambered structure occupied by homes and businesses of all shapes and sizes. The Great Tree was built into the organic framework of every beam and strut of the city. Natural leaf canopies shaded the roads, and roots plaited into bridges that crossed the rivers that flowed under and through the streets. Every shop was artisanal, no Starbucks, no McDonalds, no Walmarts. Shopkeepers traded hand-forged items and hand-grown produce. There was no poverty, no crime, no creepos wolf whistling women whenever they exposed an inch of flesh. The city had been alive, working with those who lived in her to grow and strive together.

But now, it was dead.

The Technomancer’s bombs, pushed through Ithea for miles on electrical conduits, had been mistakenly absorbed by the tree's root system. And when they had exploded in their clouds of heat and light, the tree had split and ruptured. The absence of its girth from the horizon had distressed Hallie - that much was obvious - but as she got closer, Doots noticed her demeanour change completely. And by the time they saw that the tree had fallen, dead and blackened by fire, Hallie was moving towards the city at a near sprint, eager to kill whatever was left in the second of her homes to be gutted by enemies. She ran on adrenaline and rage, and quickly left them behind to the taste of wood smoke that still lingered on the air.

By the time Doots and the rest caught up with her, she was knee deep in robotic zombie corpses, swinging The Fucker Upper with such force that a red mist surrounded her that turned out to be demon blood. Bar’Thur raised his cudgel, and Ria her pistols - ready to assist - but it was already done.

Heath brought a scanning device up and held it aloft, everyone else more than slightly suspicious of the technology he held. It pinged loudly, causing their party to jump.

“What now?” Taber hissed.

Heath raised an eyebrow. “ All lifeforms left in the city are condensed into one area. A small shack carved into the outer mountain wall, with large catacombs underneath.

“The enclave of the Mothman Propheseers.” Mookie muttered.

Doots turned to her Moth friend, she didn’t want to go back there again, the place they had butchered her mind over and over. But if it meant saving whoever was left from that ghoul, they had no choice.

Blotch, Bar’Thur, Ria Grey and Taber took point, picking off the Technomancer’s roving guards with little to no effort. They were efficient killers, and made short work of any fiends they found. Taber was a shroud of death, like that scratchy blanket your nanna has, moving unseen in shadows and tearing robots apart. Ria picked them off from the rooftops with her repeating crossbow pistols, her robotic eyes swivelling around to find new targets before the current arrow had even found its mark. She was always quiet and morose, but today she was non-communicative. Blotch and Bar’Thur were flamboyant, using any variation they could to takedown the enemy, the environment, weapons, each other. At one point Blotch stepped onto Bar’Thur’s cudgel and he launched her into a robot who she then absorbed, slowly disintegrating it in her own bodily fluids as it kicked at her shell from within. They were a whirlwind of death, and anything that got caught up in their dance was quickly destroyed. But there was a meticulousness to it, a machine-like quality. Hyper focused, efficient.

Mookie and Doots spread amongst the rubble, looking for survivors, and finding few. Whenever they found someone, a Dwarf half buried, a Goblin scavenging, Doots played a tune that only they could hear on the Keytar that led them out of the city and set them on the path towards the boats waiting at the docks.

From afar, Heath almost admired it. They were all so vehement in their defence of their people and world that it made him feel ashamed to have sat under his father’s shadow for so long. If 107 - his former friend and manservant - hadn’t bonded with him, would he have even been able to articulate these thoughts?

Hallie stood by Heath, every 100 metres or so asking if he detected the Technomancer yet. It was infuriating.

Eventually, they came to a small wooden shack sticking half out the mountain. As soon as they crossed the doorway, the scanner in Heath’s hand pinged loudly. A new energy signature. There were over 100 life forms huddled closely, and two distinctly different ones further into the complex.

Hallie took the scanner. “What is this?”

“It’s too low to be sure…but..” Heath began.

“Technomancer?” Bar’Thur growled.

Heath nodded, and immediately Hallie took the lead. She pointed to Doots, Blotch, Taber and Ria Grey. “You head towards the people we’re here to save, get them out and then rejoin the rest of us. We need to see if it’s really him. Mookie has seen him before, and Heath might be able to find a weakness of some sort. Bar’Thur’s with me for muscle.”

“Why not me?” Doots asked.

“Because you need to work out a tune to teleport those people out of here, like Heath said his version could.” Hallie knelt down as Doots returned a terrified look, putting a hand to the bard’s shoulder. “I know you can do it, just relax, like you always do. You’ll get it done. You’re great.”

Doots nodded and her and the team vanished into the darkness to free whomever was waiting down in the bowels of the catacombs.

Father Flicker, lead Seer of the Order, had been in the pit being tortured for three days now, so this new pain sat atop the old, and he barely even registered it. The same questions. Where was the Omniseer? Where was the Nexus? Where was Hallie Swiftfeather? The same response came back.

“Why don’t you suck a discarded carapace you undead fiend.”

The Technomancer slapped Flicker across the face. “Closer than you think.”

His wings had now been taken, his two hind legs burned away. He was dying, that was for certain, but he could do so with dignity and honour. As long as Mookie stayed away…but oh god there she was, on the edge of the pit, with Swiftfeather, a frog, and a robot. Unless he was delirious, he hoped so.

“Last chance, ancient thing. Where are they?”

Hallie watched the Father take hit after hit, feeling Mookie flinch against her with every blow landed. Hallie held her back, they needed to make sure the prisoners were free before they attacked. Its hunched body juddered and twitched with every word and action, horned helm shaking. It was horrible to behold, a technological litch that threatened to kill them all. Bar’thur gripped his cudgel with white knuckles, wanting to jump down and rip the ghoul apart. Heath stood still watching the thing he called a father in a different body.

“PING!”

Hallie froze, all of them staring down at the scanner at her waist. She hadn’t silenced it. The good news was that Doots had figured out how to teleport the survivors out, the bad news was that the Technomancer apparently had ears under the ram horns.

“I don’t think I need your services anymore, Flicker.” the Technomancer purred as he turned to face Hallie.

“Fly Mookie!” Father Flicker shouted before thick black tendrils ran him through the chest. He slumped, dead. Mookie screamed in protest, but it was much too late.

“Come to me, future corpses.” The Technomancer grined.

Mookie flew at the Technomancer, hitting him and somehow flying between his attacks, anticipating them. Bar’Thur and Heath followed raining blows against the monster. As Hallie landed, she drew The Fucker Upper and slashed at the technomancer’s face, but he was too fast, stabbing her deep between the lobstered armour she wore at her ribs. It was impossible to fight here, the quarters too close, their opponent too devious.

Bar’Thur was slashed across the leg severing his quadricep, and as Heath stepped in to prevent a barrage of tendrils sent to kill the Frogman, the android’s arm exploded in sparks and fizzing.

“This is so much fun.” The Technomancer’s black tongue ran across its metal teeth. “Tell me when you’ve warmed up.”

Suddenly, a golden door appeared in wall behind them and Doots, nose bleeding and looking as if she were about to collapse, appeared. “If we’re going, we go now!”

Bar’Thur hobbled through, propped by a badly damaged Heath, as Mookie grabbed Flicker’s body and flittered towards the portal.

“You go.” Hallie shouted. “I can’t let him live.”

“Not today, General.” Mookie flew at Hallie, taking them all back through the portal as Hallie screamed and fought against the momentum.

“You’ll be back, Swiftfeather! We have both seen it!” The Technomancer’s laughter echoed through the portal as it closed.

Taber had been awake for an hour before the others rose from their stupor. He was still tired, but liked the rumours that he didn’t sleep, it added to their suspicions of him. It was an new, odd feeling, to still desire to be the outsider, but to crave their acceptance. He supposed that’s what came with truly caring about people - even that Swamper Frogman he had come to call brother.

He hugged the shadow of an oak, watching whilst Ria packed her bag and left. He had contemplated asking her to stay, but the horror she had committed, even though it wasn’t her fault, was too much to bear. He understood, and said a silent prayer to the dark for her safekeeping. And when Hallie Swiftfeather got up shortly after and marched back to Etrus and her destiny, Taber still just watched. Who was he to stop her? She needed to kill the Technomancer. There was no life outside of that road for her anymore.

“Tell them I’m sorry.” Hallie said to him over her shoulder, somehow knowing he was there. “Good luck, Taber.”

He said two prayers for her. She would need them.

When Blotch woke, she made such a noise that Doots and Mookie instantly woke up too, both of them huddled together in one bedroll. Good for them, Taber thought. He watched as Blotch sit up in a daze, patting the roll next to her and looking for Bar’Thur. When her consciousness settled, and she remembered what had happened yesterday, her head hung for a moment.

“Taber, where are Hallie and Ria?” Mookie asked, walking right up to him, with none of the fear or suspicion any of the rest of them had when they talked to the Darkling..

“Both left. Ria to the north and Hallie back to her friend. There were no words to stop them.”

Mookie nodded, and helped Doots to her feet. “So what do we do?”

Blotch pulled Bar’Thur’s jerkin over half her body and picked up his cudgel. And for the first time in his long life, Taber the Darkling hesitated. “What else?” He hissed.

The Technomancer’s forces had been busy overnight, scurrying and scavenging. She was the best sword-wielder on this planet, and this ghoul was going to feel every bit of the pain it had given her, tenfold.

As she pulled the sword from its back, its head twisted 180 degrees to face her, joints cracking so that its withered limbs turned too. “Help me!” A child’s voice screamed inside the Technomancer, and Hallie for a second saw the face of a frightened young Dwarf inside it. “Quiet vessel! You have what you wanted!” The Technomancer sneered, as Hallie took an upwards swipe that cleaved the Technomancer’s face in two.

In its dying fury, its tendrils flailed wildly, one slicing clean through Hallie’s right calf. The pain drove her to the ground, blood pouring from a leg she would never use again. But at least it was dead, at least they would be safe.

A thick, oily chuckling came for the Technomancer as it rose up, its face knitting back together with machinery and wire. It strode towards her, arms morphing into long black blades. “Is that it? Tut tut tut.”

The Technomancer lunged back, and when it turned back towards Hallie, six arrow bolts were buried in its body. Ria Gray jumped down to join Hallie, and helped her rise to her feet. “Needed to kill this bastard.” She explained, and Hallie nodded.

Another Longboat flew overhead and hovered there, the noise of its engines flooding the battleground and blowing dust everywhere. A service airlock opened to reveal Blotch, who jumped out towards them. When she was about ten metres from the ground, Taber rippled out from behind her, acting as a parachute. As they both joined their friends, Blotch tutted. “Not killed him yet?”

“Waiting for us. Needed the whole gang.” Taber nodded.

Blotch charged at the Technomancer with all the speed a giant sack of jelly could, swinging the cudgel as if on the last ball of the over in the cricket world cup final at Lords Cricket Ground and the Technomancer was a slow arm bowler pitching a little too short and the field had been brought in to save runs. (Sports reference for the jock listeners). She beaned the Technomancer, denting its helm along the side with the spikes of the cudgel. When she was pushed back by the tendrils, Taber turned himself into a ball and launched himself at Blotch.

“Batter up.” He shouted - he was a fan of Wizzball, the nation’s pastime, where wizards played a magical form of baseball you’ll never see, but it is the most amazing sport you can ever imagine. Blotch hit him towards the Technomancer, and he slithered into the beast’s armour, wreaking havoc inside as he morphed into all sorts of painful looking shapes before rejoining his fellows.

Ria fired bolt after bolt at the monster, changing clips multiple times until she was out of ammunition. And when her pistols were empty, the Technomancer slowly begun healing again. Though this time, Hallie saw the body of the young Dwarf inside the closing cables, desiccated and pale.

“That’s it,” Hallie said. “The body inside.”

Ria grabbed The Fucker Upper from Hallie and ran at the healing fiend, only for it to grab the sword when the blade was only a foot from the body inside. It snapped the sword in two with his bare hands, and shoved a shard of it into Ria’s stomach. He held her aloft as the light went from her eyes, then chucked her dead body to one side.

“Two down.” The chrome skull said.

Taber and Blotch ran at the murderer, fighting back the incoming tendrils as they rushed forward. When they got close, they pulled the cables to one side and Hallie jumped with all the strength she had left. Swordless, she buried the pointed edge of her shield, Steve, into the heart of Timmy Blackcrest. With a scream, the Technomancer shedded away from the corpse of the Dwarf and fell to the floor, Timmy collapsing on the spot. If he were still able, he would have thanked them.

“I would say something witty, but we have to get out of here now.” Taber pointed towards the army of zombified robots scurrying towards them. Taber turned himself into a long set of black stairs that they climbed to the Longboat, taking the body of Ria Grey with them. As the ship started moving slowly away from the dying planet of Ithia, Hallie turned back to the battlefield…and found the Technomancer still lived.

The shroud the Technomancer had become, a wraith of hate and evil manifested in an AI named Eden, rose and glared at her. “No.” The thing screamed. As it raised her arms, the advancing dead bodies burrowed themselves into the Earth and rose at the Technomancer’s feet. They became a rising tidal wave that lifted the Technomancer at its head. It was rising fast, and it was going to engulf the Longboat.

“Doots. Portal!” Mookie asked, and Doots picked her Keytar up, as weak as she was, she started playing.

In front of the ship, a golden portal in space appeared, then immediately cracked into splinters, like a broken gilded mirror. “I don’t know what’s happening. It’s still open, but someone, somewhere has broken it.” Doots cried.

“It doesn’t matter none, Doots. It’s going to catch us.” Blotch said. “It was fun guys. You know, except for all the death and stuff.”

Hallie looked at the portal, the rising dead, and her friends. This was it, what she had seen in her visions, a chance to save the people she loved. “I really fucking hate that thing.” She said to Mookie, before she leaped from the Longboat to the screaming protests of her family, holding the shield in front like a ram. As she careened into the Technomancer, she knocked it off balance, and gave the ship the last few seconds it needed to reach the portal. Before falling back to the planet she had loved so much.

The Technomancer rushed towards the portal and crossed the threshold, only to arrive somewhere she didn’t know, with no longboat in sight. She let out a scream of frustration at the escape of her quarry, before taking a deep breath and realising she would find them, it would just take more of what she had an abundance of now.

Time.
