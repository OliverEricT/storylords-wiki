“Temporal sensors suggest 1580s Earth.” HUM-13 declared. “The total theft of North America by the Europeans from those native to these lands.” HUM-13 looked at the camera long and hard.

“They look extremely agitated for people who have just gotten away with wholesale land larceny.” Oryx looked back, realising the plane was parked on a piece of wood. He tore it from the wheel and held the sign in front of him. “Roanoak…” he muttered, before looking at HUM-13, who shrugged.

“No. I won’t let you. I’ll do it, I’ll go back.” Kellan begged.

Malcolm smiled. “No, you won’t. You’ll stay exactly where you are, out of the way.”

“I don’t get it,” said Kellan. “You wanted me to go back - you finally convince me to give up a chance at normality - and then you refuse?”

“Ain’t I a stinker?” Malcolm smirked, his large fangs glinting the light back at Kellan.

Lunging across the table, Kellan went to attack him, but he slithered away around the table. He laugh-howled. “Grab me? Was that your grand plan? I’m a god for hell's sake, not your kindergarten boyfriend. What were you going to do, noogie me, maybe gimme a mean swirly?”

“I need to go back, and you’re not going to stop me.” Kellan said opening a Rift where Malcolm was, but he was gone. She felt a panting at her shoulder, before his tongue wrapped around her and threw her across the room.

“Gawd, you’re annoying! You ain’t never happy, girl. This way you get to stay hidden away and play happy families with your sister. And all it costs you is everything else you ever knew, every friend you had, and any answers you ever wanted about your real parents…” He put a taloned, lizard paw to his expression of mock surprise, causing Kellan to stop dead in her tracks. “Oooops, did I say that?” He pulled a piece of paper from behind her ear, party magician style. “Maybe the next step in getting your answers is on this scrap of paper. And maybe, if you sit still and let what’s gotta happen happen. I’ll give it to you at the end, as a closing text crawl for the end of your world.”

Kellan shook her head. “No, you don’t know that, nobody does. Nobody could…”

“Nobody except maybe the gods?” Malcolm smirked.

“You’re no, god, you’re just another mangy mutt.” Kellan pulled a small, yellow cube from her pocket and threw it in the air. She watched as Malcolm’s nose twitched, and his eyes closed. He wafted in the air after the yellow cube like Pepe Le Pew on his way to sexually assault a cat with white paint on it, or frat boys sniffing out your horny, sex starved mother before they never call her again.

“If’n I got a weakness, it’s a bit of cheese.”

“...it’s a bit of cheese.”

“...BIT OF CHEESE.”

The echo was almost tangible in the room, and who could say it wasn’t, and not just because you all need reminding of the throwaway line I used to shoehorn in this prompt this week. With Malcolm following his treat, Kellan opened Rift after Rift in the room, so many that even he couldn’t dodge them. He was instantly severed in two, thick black viscous blood oozing out of the open wound. Both parts dropped to the floor and he hissed and spat like a dying monster. “You cut me in two! You know how hard it’s going to be to find one of those doggie jackets that fit now? And winter is coming man! You could have at least Old Yeller’d me like a human being.”

She snatched the piece of paper from his paw and opened it. It simply said a time and a place. A crackle of electricity stood her hair on end as a Rift opened before her. The realisation of what she was leaving behind hit her. But she knew that Ash would know what to do to keep herself safe. Still, fuck him for making her do this. With one last fury filled look at Malcolm, she stepped through the portal.

After she was gone, Malcolm sat up and beckoned his lower half towards him, and watched as his back end knitted itself back onto his front. “Neato.” He said with a sick smile.

“More enjoyable than anything else occurring parallel to our predicament, I wager. Well if they want excitement they should really give more feedback.” Oryx said completely stone faced and unambiguously. “Humie? Why didn’t you let me be Nessie?”

“This next confrontation should be most entertaining,” said Oryx as he hunched forward in his chair. “Do we have any snacks?”

“His competitor has apparently won the last two bouts she has competed in. Her gauntlets produce so much heat they can cleave steel in two.” HUM-13 declared. “This is an anonymous contest, but the crowd have dubbed her ‘Laser Fister’.”

“Woooooo, we love you, Laser Fister!” A member of the crowd screamed. “Have my babies!”

“First to enter the ring of pain, a Norn/Velitean who, though anonymous, will need no introduction.”
